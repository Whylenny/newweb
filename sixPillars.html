The Six Pillars

They all work together as improvements to one will improve the others.

Eat More Fiber

There are very few cases of protein deficiency (kwashiorkor) but over 90% of Americans are not getting enough fiber. The minimum recommended amount is 25 - 30 grams but the average American is eating only 15 grams.

The fiber is actually not digestible! In fact it is food for the microbiome - the microbes that live in the large intestine. In exchange for providing them fiber they provide us many health benefits. They are the main source of serotonin (the happiness neurotransmitter).

Less is More


Food processing ranges from washing your blueberries to having your cereal "shot from guns."

Food processing such as grinding or juicing breaks down the fiber which raises the rate at which the calories in the food get into your bloodstream (the glycemic index). If the fiber stays intact the calories are not even be absorbed by you but by your microbiome.



Fermented Foods are Prebiotic and Probiotic

The bacteria and/or yeast that ferments the food is beneficial. These organisims will reside in your gut to continue to provide neurotransmitters, vitamins, and short-chain fatty acids to improve your health.

Fermented Foods include Kimchi, Sauerkraut, Yogurt, Kefir, Kombucha, Miso, Tempeh and Dill Pickles. Besides providing healthy microbes for your gut, these foods also promote the growth of other beneficial microbes and increases the variety of organisms in your biome.

Avoiding Risky Substances

Risky substances can damage your body and many of them may be addictive as well. You may not be able to eliminate these substances from your life but each time you opt out is a win for your health.

We are all aware of the increased risk of heart disease and cancer from tobacco use. It took more than 4000 studies proving this link before the surgeon general's warning was placed on tobacco products and use of tobacco started to decline.

It was many years later that the tobacco companies finally admitted that nicotine was addictive. During that time scientists hired by the tobacco companies created lots of erroneous studies questioning the harmfulness of tobacco.

Since then the tobacco companies have bought up food production companies and are now using the same tactics to promote their food products.

Oil is Calorie Dense

That's why we like it. In times of scarcity it is important to eat enough and our bodies reward us when we consume high calorie foods. (Dopamine is secreted which we experience as pleaseure.) We also like to feel full but oil doesn't give that feeling.


In times of plenty there is a danger of getting too much oil. Eating too much oil (and fat) can:

. weaken the intestinal lining eventually leading to "leaky gut"

. relax the lower exophageal sphincter (entrance to the stomach) leading to heartburn and acid reflux

. impair insulin's ability to transport sucrose into the muscle cells leading to high blood sugar and type 2 diabetes.

Oil has more calories per ounce than any other food.
